Q2 Grading Rubric

You might notice that as easy as it is to pass, it's also very easy to fail. The requirements are not hard. You are also not being graded on the quality of the product as of this point in time. However, do note that the grading method I'm using is designed to push you all to be more responsible as we move forward. Q1 grades were given based on completion. Q2 grades are being given based on whether you're actually practicing what's instructed in order to improve your artistic ability. Q3 grades will mark the beginning of grading for quality of the assignment. Q4 will be more or less like Q3, but you will be graded based on both quality and improvement.

Grade Description
100 The student followed the directions and used the drawing methods demonstrated to achieve the goal of the assignment.
85 The student followed the directions and demonstrated an understanding of the drawing methods shown in class, but the assignment that was turned in was incomplete.
70 The student began by following the directions and using the methods demonstrated but decided somewhere along the line to veer off course. For example: In the perspective drawings, someone may have started by using their straight-edge tool to create the lines that go toward the vanishing point, but ended up giving up midway through and drawing the rest in a freehand manner. Student did not follow directions but turned in the assignment.
50 There is evidence of cheating/plagiarism, most likely through the method of tracing instead of doing what was instructed. While this would normally be a 0, the student still "created" and turned in something, which is better than doing nothing at all.
0 `Nothing was turned in, whatsoever. OR Because some students were abusing the fact that they could get a 70 without having to follow directions, you will now receive a failing grade for not following the instructions of the assignment. If you're not going to try at all, that's now going to be treated the same as turning nothing in.

Syllabus

Current Standard

15. Create two-dimensional and three-dimensional sketches, including rough and refined sketches, demonstrating shape, volume, depth, and dimension. Distinguish among common illustration techniques used in design composition such as one-point, two-point, and multi-point perspective drawings. Develop conceptual design ideas using freehand sketching. For a given design problem, generate, analyze, and refine sketches to develop design solutions. Use the sketches to create refined drawings utilizing design software. For example, create thumbnail sketches to generate ideas for a logo or advertisement. (TN Reading 2, 3, 4, 7; TN Writing 4, 6, 7, 8, 9)

  • Students will create a "baseline," preliminary drawing that establishes their initial skill level before learning the technical approach to perspective illustration. This drawing will then be used to compare their progress as we move forward through the second quarter of the academic year.

Oct. 24 - 28 One-Point Perspective Week
  • Students will be provided a demonstration on the basics of one point perspective. This will include the explanations of any relevant terms, such as vanishing point, horizon line, and radial lines, and their relevance to the subject matter.
  • Following the demonstration, students will be given an image prompt that they will then use to practice a one-point perspective drawing.
  • After being provided feedback on their initial illustration, students will then be given the opportunity to create a second illustation, with the aim of showing improvement compared to the first one.
  • Main Assignment: Students will use the feedback given to them after their initial attempt at one-point perspective to recreate the hallway scene given to them in their image prompt. For homework, students will create a one-point perspective illustration of a real world location of their own choosing. (Classwork/Homework)
  • Secondary Assignment: Students will practice one-point perspective techniques using the information given to them and the image prompt in order to identify areas of artistic strength and weakness.
 Main Assignment: Oct. 28, 2022 (Classwork), Nov. 4, 2022 (Homework)
Secondary Assignment: Oct. 28, 2022
Oct. 31 - Nov. 4 Two-Point Perspective Week
  • Students will be introduced to the two-point perspective drawing technique. A demonstration will be given that illustrates how to use two point perspective for both interior and exterior drawings.
  • Following the demonstration, students will be given the opportunity to practice two-point perspective based on the image prompt given. Feedback will be given after this point.
  • After being provided feedback, students will utilize the information provided to them in order to create a more accurate illustration.
  • Students will then be tasked with drawing a location of their own choosing that demonstrates two-point perspective from an interior viewpoint in order to show their understanding.
  • Main Assignment: Students will create two (2) two-point perspective drawings demonstrating their understanding of the artistic method. One of the drawings will be done in class, based on the given image prompt. The other will be done for homework, based on a location of each student's individual choosing. (Classwork/Homework)
 Main Assignment: Nov. 4, 2022 (Classwork), Nov. 11, 2022 (Homework)
