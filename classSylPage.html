Following this model, as of 2022-08-28, all assignments given will have an indicator of what type of grade they account for. However, all students should note that the sketchbook assignments will account for both homework and assessment grades, resulting in being 50% of your total grade for each quarter. Because there is a long time between when I first announced the requirement of the sketchbook and the first official due date for sketchbook assignments, there will be no leniency for anyone who doesn't have it by September 30. Furthermore, as far as this class page is concerned, all week-long assignments will be referred to as the Main Assignment in the syllabus table. All sketchbook assignments will be referred to as the Secondary Assignment.

Syllabus

Date Description Due Date
Aug. 8 - 12 Intro week. N/A
Aug. 15 - 19 Logo Week
  • Students will learn the difference between a logotype and a logomark.
  • Students will learn the five traits that make up a successful logo.
  • Students will learn the seven Gestalt principles of design.
  • Students will learn the difference between plagiarism and appropriation.
  • Main Assignment: Students will sketch an idea for a logo.
  • Secondary Assignment: Students will define the seven Gestalt principles of design and create a sketch representing at least two of the seven Gestalt principles of design.
 Main Assignment: 2nd day of class for the week.
Secondary Assignment: Sep. 30
Aug. 22 - 26 Typography Week
  • Students will learn the difference between serif, sans-serif, and script fonts.
  • Students will define kerning and spacing as it relates to typography and design.
  • Students will learn the basics of typographical arrangements.
  • Main Assignment: Unable to assign due to lack of software access. Will be moved to a later date, TBD.
  • Secondary Assignment: Students will sketch at least three different typographical arrangments.
 Main Assignment: N/A
Secondary Assignment: Sep. 30
Aug. 29 - Sep 2 Color Week
  • Students will learn the basics of color theory.
  • Students will learn the difference between tints, shades, and chromatic grays.
  • Students will learn the difference between the color models: RGB, Hex, HSB/HSL/HSV, and CMYK.
  • Students will learn the difference between the various color schemes: complementary, analagous, harmonious, etc.
  • Main Assignment:
    • Students will demonstrate a basic understanding of digital art tools by creating a color palette that shows primary colors, the RGB color model, secondary colors, and chromatic grays. (Classwork)
    • Students will demonstrate an understanding of basic color usage by creating a series of three (3) separate posters/photos that are manipulated using digital art software to change the overall color scheme of each. If the student so wishes, there is also the option of placing one image/icon on a digital canvas, duplicating it twice, and altering the color of each copy, independently, so that there are three versions of varied color. Either option will fulfill the conditions of the assignment. (Classwork)
    • In order to complete these assignments, students will need to make use of digital art software. Ibis Paint X is currently the one in use for assignment tutorials as of right now, as it is free on the iOS and Android app stores. However, if you are familiar with and are currently able to use a similar software to achieve the goals of the assignment, you are free to use another software. For instruction this week, refer to the class resources at the end of the page. If the videos are not up by Monday morning, 8/29, refer to your secondary assignment, and be on the lookout for the main assignment on this page later in the day. As of writing this, there is currently a technical error going on with the video editing software that needs to be addressed.
  • Secondary Assignment: Students will define color vocabulary in their sketchbooks. Vocabulary to be included in the assignment are: hue, saturation, value, tint, shade, chromatic, achromatic, monochromatic, primary colors, secondary colors, chromatic gray, complement, split complement, analogous colors, harmony, dissonance, triad, tetrad, CMYK, RGB, HSV, and hex codes.
 Main Assignment: First class of the next week.
Secondary Assignment: Sep. 30
Sep. 6 - Sep. 9 Basics of Photoshop
  • Students will learn the names and functions of the basic tools in Adobe Photoshop.
  • Students will learn the basics of Photoshop features.
  • Students will learn how to implement Photoshop's layering features to compose imagery.
  • Students will learn the use of the opacity features in Photoshop.
  • Main Assignment: Students will create an image composition demonstrating the use of layers.
  • Secondary Assignment: Students will create a sketch demonstrating the basics of layering.
 Main Assignment: First class of the next week.
Secondary Assignment: Sep. 30
Sep. 12 - 16 Blend Modes Week
  • Students will learn how to utilize the overlay, multiply, and subtract layer blending modes.
  • Students will learn the application of blending modes to achieve a variety of color and shading effects.
Sep. 19 - 23 Layer Effects Week
  • Students will learn to utilize three basic layer effects: border, glow, and drop shadow.
  • Utilizing text as the primary subjects, students will experiment with the different learned layer effects to create typographical art.
Sep. 26 - 30 Photo Effects Week
  • Students will learn to manipulate photos utilizing the blur, vignette, posterize, and sharpen effects.
  • Utilizing a photo of themselves or a chosen subject, students will demonstrate their capability to use photo effects to enhance or alter photos for visual appeal.
Oct. 3 - 7 Selections and Masking Week
  • Students will learn how to use the marquee tools, magic wand, selection layers, and selection paths in order to manipulate specific areas or colors within a project.
  • Students will learn to hide or show specific parts of an image using layer masks.
  • Students will learn to manipulate specifics elements on a layer by using the alpha lock option.
Oct. 10 - 14 Fall Break N/A
Oct. 17 - 21 Editing and Correction Tools
  • Students will learn to utilize the different photo correction tools, dodge, burn, smudge, and blur to edit photographs.
Oct. 24 - 28 Tonal Correction
  • Students will learn to utilize tonal correction sliders to adjust different properties about an image.
