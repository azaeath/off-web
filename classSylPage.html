Syllabus

Current Standard(s) of Assessment

22. Apply the design process to complete projects of increasing complexity and of varying applications such as print, web, film, and marketing communications. Demonstrate the ability to select and use the appropriate tools and procedures to accomplish project goals. Prepare an informative narrative to explain a design to a peer, emphasizing how the design process and the design elements and principles were applied.

23. Utilize the critique and refinement strategy as part of the design process to achieve project goals. As part of a design project, present preliminary design ideas in a way that is understandable to an audience using both visual and verbal explanations. Note constructive criticism received and use it to influence design refinement. Similarly, evaluate the work of others, drawing on design principles and project goals, to provide clear, specific, and constructive feedback.

Oct. 17 - 21 Intro to Perspective Week
  • Students will create a "baseline," preliminary drawing that establishes their initial skill level before learning the technical approach to perspective illustration. This drawing will then be used to compare their progress as we move forward through the second quarter of the academic year.
  • Students will be given feedback in order to address what they are doing well compared to areas in which they can improve. After addressing what the students strengths and weaknesses are, they will be instructed in the methodology used to draw from sight with more accuracy, in order to create a more believable illustration.
  • Following instructions about the methods to create a believable illustration, the students will then use the techniques demonstrated to them to create a secondary illustration with the aim of improving, even if only to a minor degree, their drawing accuracy.
  • Main Assignment: Using the methods demonstrated to you in class, create a drawing that establishes the location of the objects in the given image with as much accuracy as you're able to at this point in time. (Classwork)
    • Note that this is not a freehand drawing. To do this properly, you will need to use a ruler or a straight edge tool of some kind to estabilsh your lines.
  • Secondary Assignment: Create your initial drawing of the given subject matter, preferably in your sketchbook, and provide it in some manner that allows for feedback to be given. (Class Participation)

Oct. 24 - 28 One-Point Perspective Week
  • Students will be provided a demonstration on the basics of one point perspective. This will include the explanations of any relevant terms, such as vanishing point, horizon line, and radial lines, and their relevance to the subject matter.
  • Following the demonstration, students will be given an image prompt that they will then use to practice a one-point perspective drawing.
  • After being provided feedback on their initial illustration, students will then be given the opportunity to create a second illustation, with the aim of showing improvement compared to the first one.
  • Main Assignment: Students will use the feedback given to them after their initial attempt at one-point perspective to recreate the hallway scene given to them in their image prompt. For homework, students will create a one-point perspective illustration of a real world location of their own choosing. (Classwork/Homework)
  • Secondary Assignment: Students will practice one-point perspective techniques using the information given to them and the image prompt in order to identify areas of artistic strength and weakness.
 Main Assignment: Oct. 28, 2022 (Classwork), Nov. 4, 2022 (Homework)
Secondary Assignment: Oct. 28, 2022
Oct. 31 - Nov. 4 Two-Point Perspective Week
  • Students will be introduced to the two-point perspective drawing technique. A demonstration will be given that illustrates how to use two point perspective for both interior and exterior drawings.
  • Following the demonstration, students will be given the opportunity to practice two-point perspective based on the image prompt given. Feedback will be given after this point.
  • After being provided feedback, students will utilize the information provided to them in order to create a more accurate illustration.
  • Students will then be tasked with drawing a location of their own choosing that demonstrates two-point perspective from an interior viewpoint in order to show their understanding.
  • Main Assignment: Students will create two (2) two-point perspective drawings demonstrating their understanding of the artistic method. One of the drawings will be done in class, based on the given image prompt. The other will be done for homework, based on a location of each student's individual choosing. (Classwork/Homework)
 Main Assignment: Nov. 4, 2022 (Classwork), Nov. 11, 2022 (Homework)
